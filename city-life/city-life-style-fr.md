# City Life — France Style

French counterpart to the German diorama in `map-2.png`. Same rendering style,
French architecture and city fabric. This file is both the visual style guide
and the image-generation prompt. Companion to `city-life-style.md` (Japan) and
`city-life-style-cl.md` (Chile).

Mostly **Strasbourg / Alsatian**, with **two districts kept Parisian-grand**:
the train station and the formal park.

## Shared style (keep identical to the German map)

- **3D isometric miniature / tilt-shift diorama**, high angle, vertical frame.
- **Warm dusk light**, long soft shadows, windows just starting to glow.
- Muted, slightly desaturated palette; toy-model / architectural-maquette feel.
- A **river** crossing the scene with bridges.
- Clear **districts** so each structure reads as its own place.

## What changes for France (Strasbourg-led)

The defining fabric is **Alsatian Strasbourg**: colorful **half-timbered
(colombage) houses** with steep pointed roofs and flower boxes, **pink
sandstone** civic buildings, and the **Ill river branching into canals** (La
Petite France) crossed by wooden **covered bridges (Ponts Couverts)**. The
landmark is **Strasbourg Cathedral** — a single tall asymmetric Gothic spire in
pink sandstone (replaces the German domed cathedral).

Two districts stay **Parisian** for contrast and grandeur: the monumental
**gare** and the **formal French garden**.

## Layout suggestion (mirror the German composition)

- **Top edge:** airport + runway.
- **Upper-middle:** the grand Parisian gare with its arched glass train shed.
- **The Ill across the middle**, branching into canals with wooden covered bridges.
- **Center:** Alsatian half-timbered old town; Strasbourg Cathedral's single spire rising over it.
- **One side:** La Petite France canal quarter.
- **Lower blocks:** winstub lane, the formal Parisian jardin, Palais Rohan, hospital.
