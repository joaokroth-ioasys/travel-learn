# City Life — Chile Style

Chilean counterpart to the German diorama in `map-2.png`. Same **rendering
style**, but a deliberately different **city** — organized by the climb toward
the Andes, not by a river. This file is both the visual style guide and the
image-generation prompt. Companion to `city-life-style.md` (Japan) and
`city-life-style-fr.md` (France).

Fuses **Valparaíso** (colorful cerros, funiculars) and **Santiago** (colonial
center, La Moneda, Gran Torre).

## Shared style (keep identical to the German map)

- **3D isometric miniature / tilt-shift diorama**, high angle, vertical frame.
- **Warm dusk light**, long soft shadows, windows just starting to glow.
- Muted, slightly desaturated palette; toy-model / architectural-maquette feel.
- Clear **districts** so each structure reads as its own place.

## What changes for Chile (and how it stops looking German)

No river, no formal fountain park, no central cathedral spine. The composition
is a **slope / altitude gradient**, fully inland:

**dense low blocks + feria (bottom) → flat colonial center → colorful cerros
climbed by funiculars → snow-capped Andes on the high horizon (top).**

The **Andes** are the anchor the German river used to be. City fabric is
terracotta and pastel façades, yellow *micros/colectivos* on the streets, and a
mural or two on the cerro walls.

## The 7 structures (Chilean counterparts)

| # | Structure | Chilean rendering |
|---|-----------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Airport** | Modern airport on a flat outskirt — long runway, glass terminal, planes at gates, Andes rising behind. |
| 2 | **Train station** | Historic **Estación Central**: a long wrought-iron-and-glass hall (Eiffel-workshop style), a Metro entrance, a plaza with palm trees. |
| 3 | **Restaurant** | A **feria libre** cluster: rows of colorful market tarps, empanada and completo carts, picada/parrilla tables spilling into the street. Replaces the German café row. |
| 4 | **Hospital** | Clean modern hospital block, pale façade, red cross, small ambulance bay. |
| 5 | **Tourist guide** | The landmark: a **cerro** of colorful stacked houses with a **funicular (ascensor)** climbing to a lookout crowned by a white statue (Virgen del Cerro San Cristóbal). The sightseeing anchor — replaces the cathedral. |
| 6 | **Gym / park** | **Cerro Santa Lucía**: a green rocky hill with neoclassical stone terraces, fountains, palm trees and winding paths. Optional small gym at its base. |
| 7 | **Public services** | **La Moneda**: a low neoclassical government palace with inner courtyards, flags and a formal forecourt, beside a colonial **Plaza de Armas** with palms and a cathedral. |

## Layout (the altitude gradient — no water, no German mirror)

- **Far horizon (top):** snow-capped Andes across the whole width.
- **Rising band below the Andes:** the colorful cerros with funiculars and the statue lookout; Cerro Santa Lucía's terraced park.
- **Mid band (flat center):** Plaza de Armas with palms + colonial cathedral, La Moneda, and a small **Gran Torre / Costanera** glass-tower cluster.
- **Lower flat band:** airport + runway on one outskirt; Estación Central and its palm-lined plaza.
- **Low edge (bottom):** the feria libre and dense terracotta city blocks. No river, no sea.
