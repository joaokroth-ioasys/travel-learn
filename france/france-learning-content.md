# French Travel Learning: Content Structure (A1)

---

## Travel Route

**Entry point:** Paris (CDG airport)
**Final destination:** Brussels (Belgium)

```
Paris (CDG) --> Strasbourg --> Lyon --> Nice --> Marseille
                                                     |
Brussels <-- Lille <-- Nantes <-- Bordeaux <-- Toulouse
```

| Leg | From | To | Distance | Train time |
|-----|------|----|----------|------------|
| 1 | Paris | Strasbourg | 490 km | 1h 47m (TGV) |
| 2 | Strasbourg | Lyon | 490 km | 3h 30m (TGV) |
| 3 | Lyon | Nice | 470 km | 4h 45m |
| 4 | Nice | Marseille | 200 km | 2h 30m |
| 5 | Marseille | Toulouse | 405 km | 3h 30m |
| 6 | Toulouse | Bordeaux | 245 km | 2h 05m (TGV) |
| 7 | Bordeaux | Nantes | 325 km | 2h 15m |
| 8 | Nantes | Lille | 620 km | 3h 30m (via Paris) |
| 9 | Lille | Brussels | 105 km | 35m (Eurostar/Thalys) |

**Story arc:** A complete beginner arrives in France speaking no French. Armed with enthusiasm and a phrasebook, they travel the country by train, picking up survival French city by city. By Brussels, they can introduce themselves, order food, ask for directions, tell the time, describe things, and talk about their plans.

## Chapter 1: Paris
**Story:** The traveler lands at Charles de Gaulle Airport, takes the RER B train into the city, and checks into a small hotel in the Latin Quarter. Their first challenge: communicate without speaking any French.

**Vocabulary Objectives:** Airport and transport, greetings, introductions, numbers 1-12
**Grammar Objective:** Subject pronouns + etre (to be) in the present tense

---

### Lesson 1.1: Arriving in France (Arriver en France)

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| l'aeroport (m) | airport |
| le vol | flight |
| l'arrivee (f) | arrival |
| le passeport | passport |
| la douane | customs |
| les bagages (m pl) | luggage |
| le train | train |
| le billet | ticket |
| la sortie | exit |
| a gauche | to the left |
| a droite | to the right |
| tout droit | straight ahead |
| pardon | excuse me / sorry |
| s'il vous plait | please (formal) |
| merci | thank you |

**Key Phrases**
- "Ou sont les bagages ?" - Where is the luggage?
- "Un billet pour Paris, s'il vous plait." - One ticket to Paris, please.
- "C'est quelle sortie ?" - Which exit is it?
- "Pardon, ou est le train ?" - Excuse me, where is the train?

**Grammar: Subject Pronouns + etre (to be)**

etre is the first and most essential verb in French. It is completely irregular.

| Pronoun | etre | Meaning |
|---------|------|---------|
| je | suis | I am |
| tu | es | you are (informal) |
| il / elle / on | est | he / she / one is |
| nous | sommes | we are |
| vous | etes | you are (formal or plural) |
| ils / elles | sont | they are |

- "Je suis en France !" - I am in France!
- "Elle est de Paris." - She is from Paris.
- "Nous sommes a l'aeroport." - We are at the airport.
- "C'est le train pour Paris." - It is the train to Paris. (c'est = it is)

Note: **tu** is informal (friends, family, children). **vous** is formal (strangers, older people, service staff) or when addressing a group.

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| l'hotel (m) | hotel |
| la chambre | room |
| une chambre simple | single room |
| une chambre double | double room |
| la cle / la carte | key / key card |

| j'ai une reservation | I have a reservation |
| pour combien de nuits ? | for how many nights? |

**Key Phrases**
- "J'ai une reservation au nom de Silva." - I have a reservation under the name Silva.
- "Une chambre double, s'il vous plait." - A double room, please.
- "C'est combien par nuit ?" - How much is it per night?
- "Le petit-dejeuner est inclus ?" - Is breakfast included?
- "Le Wi-Fi est gratuit ?" - Is Wi-Fi free?

**Grammar: Definite Articles (le, la, l', les)**

French nouns have grammatical gender (masculine or feminine). The article signals the gender.

| | Singular | Plural |
|--|---------|--------|
| Masculine | le (before consonant) | les |
| Feminine | la (before consonant) | les |
| Before vowel or silent h | l' (both genders) | les |

- le train, le billet, le passeport (masculine)
- la chambre, la cle, la sortie (feminine)
- l'hotel, l'aeroport, l'arrivee (before vowel)
- les bagages, les billets, les chambres (plural)

**Definite** (the) = le/la/l'/les: refers to something specific or already known.
**Indefinite** (a / some) = un/une/des: refers to something non-specific.

| | Singular | Plural |
|--|---------|--------|
| Masculine | un | des |
| Feminine | une | des |

- "Je voudrais **une** chambre." (a room - first mention)
- "Voici **la** chambre." (the room - this specific one)

## Chapter 3: Lyon
**Story:** The traveler takes the TGV south to Lyon, France's gastronomic capital. They discover the bouchons (Lyon's legendary traditional restaurants) and must order their first proper French meal entirely on their own.

**Vocabulary Objectives:** Food and drink, restaurant, ordering, paying
**Grammar Objective:** Regular -er verbs in the present tense

---

### Lesson 3.1: At the Bouchon (Au Bouchon)

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| le restaurant | restaurant |
| la carte / le menu | menu (a la carte / set menu) |
| l'entree (f) | starter |
| le plat principal | main course |
| le dessert | dessert |
| la quenelle | quenelle (Lyon fish dumpling specialty) |
| le gratin dauphinois | potato gratin with cream |
| le fromage | cheese |
| le pain | bread |
| la soupe | soup |
| la viande | meat |
| le poisson | fish |
| les legumes (m pl) | vegetables |
| vegetarien(ne) | vegetarian |
| sans | without |

**Key Phrases**
- "Une table pour deux, s'il vous plait." - A table for two, please.
- "Vous avez la carte, s'il vous plait ?" - Can we have the menu, please?
- "Je voudrais le plat principal avec des legumes." - I would like the main course with vegetables.
- "C'est quoi, les quenelles ?" - What are quenelles?
- "Je suis vegetarien(ne)." - I am vegetarian.
- "L'addition, s'il vous plait !" - The bill, please!

**Grammar: Regular -er Verbs (Present Tense)**

The majority of French verbs end in -er. Remove -er and add these endings:

| Pronoun | Ending | manger (to eat) | parler (to speak) |
|---------|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| je | -e | mange | parle |
| tu | -es | manges | parles |
| il/elle | -e | mange | parle |
| nous | -ons | mangeons | parlons |
| vous | -ez | mangez | parlez |
| ils/elles | -ent | mangent | parlent |

Note: je, tu, il/elle endings sound identical (-e, -es, -e are all silent). ils/elles -ent is also silent. Only -ons and -ez are audible.

Note: **manger** adds an extra e before -ons (mangeons) to keep the soft "g" sound.

- "Je mange une quenelle." - I eat a quenelle.
- "Vous parlez anglais ?" - Do you speak English?
- "Nous mangeons ensemble." - We eat together.

## Chapter 4: Nice
**Story:** The traveler heads to the Cote d'Azur. Nice is vibrant, colorful, and sunny. They spend a day on the Promenade des Anglais and visit the Cours Saleya flower and food market. Their challenge: describe the world around them.

**Vocabulary Objectives:** Weather, colors, basic descriptions, beach and nature
**Grammar Objective:** Adjective agreement (gender and number)

---

### Lesson 4.1: The Weather and Colors (La Meteo et les Couleurs)

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| le soleil | sun |
| il fait beau | the weather is nice |
| il fait chaud | it is hot |
| il fait froid | it is cold |
| il pleut | it is raining |
| il neige | it is snowing |
| nuageux/nuageuse | cloudy |
| le vent | wind |
| il y a du vent | it is windy |
| la plage | beach |
| la mer | sea |
| bleu(e) | blue |
| rouge | red |
| vert(e) | green |
| jaune | yellow |

**Key Phrases**
- "Il fait beau aujourd'hui !" - The weather is lovely today!
- "Il fait trop chaud." - It is too hot.
- "La mer est bleue et le ciel est bleu." - The sea is blue and the sky is blue.
- "Quel temps fait-il ?" - What is the weather like?
- "J'adore la plage !" - I love the beach!

**Grammar: Adjective Agreement (Gender)**

In French, adjectives agree in gender with the noun they describe.

**Feminine formation - key rules:**

| Rule | Masculine | Feminine |
|------|-----------|---------|
| add -e | petit | petite |
| already ends in -e | rouge | rouge (no change) |
| double consonant + e | bon | bonne |
| -er becomes -ere | premier | premiere |
| -eux becomes -euse | heureux | heureuse |
| irregular | beau | belle |

**Colors with agreement:**
- un sac rouge / une voiture rouge (rouge stays the same)
- un ciel bleu / une mer bleue
- un jardin vert / une plante verte
- un mur blanc / une porte blanche (blanc becomes blanche)

**Adjective placement:** most adjectives go AFTER the noun.
- "La mer bleue." - The blue sea.

BAGS adjectives (Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size) go BEFORE: beau/belle, vieux/vieille, bon/bonne, grand/grande, petit/petite, jeune, nouveau/nouvelle.
- "Une belle plage." - A beautiful beach.
- "Un petit cafe." - A small cafe.

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| le marche | market |

| la bouillabaisse | bouillabaisse (Marseille fish stew) |
| les fruits (m pl) | fruit |
| les legumes (m pl) | vegetables |
| le fromage | cheese |

**Key Phrases**
- "Je voudrais un kilo de tomates, s'il vous plait." - I would like a kilo of tomatoes, please.
- "C'est combien le fromage ?" - How much is the cheese?
- "Vous avez des fraises ?" - Do you have strawberries?
- "Je prends ca." - I'll take that.
- "Vous avez quelque chose de moins cher ?" - Do you have something cheaper?

**Grammar: Partitive Articles (du / de la / de l' / des)**

Use partitive articles to express "some" or an unspecified quantity of something uncountable.

| Gender | Before consonant | Before vowel or silent h |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Masculine | du (= de + le) | de l' |
| Feminine | de la | de l' |
| Plural | des | des |

- "Je mange **du** pain." (some bread - masculine)
- "Je bois **de l'**eau gazeuse." (some sparkling water - before vowel)
- "Il achete **des** fruits." (some fruit - plural)
- "Elle mange **du** fromage et **des** olives."

**Important rule:** after negation, du/de la/de l'/des all become **de** (d' before a vowel):
- "Je mange du pain." -> "Je **ne** mange **pas de** pain."
- "Il y a des oranges." -> "Il **n'**y a **pas d'**oranges."

### Lesson 5.2: Prices and Negation (Les Prix et la Negation)

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| l'euro (m) | euro |
| les centimes | cents |
| ca coute | it costs |
| la monnaie | change / coins |
| le billet | banknote |
| la carte bancaire | bank card |
| un recu | receipt |
| acheter | to buy |
| vendre | to sell |
| pas de... | no / not any... |
| rien | nothing |
| jamais | never |
| plus | no more / not anymore |
| encore | again / more |
| pouvez-vous repeter ? | can you repeat? |

**Key Phrases**
- "Ca coute cinq euros cinquante." - It costs five euros fifty.
- "Je n'ai pas de monnaie." - I don't have change.
- "Je ne parle pas francais." - I don't speak French.
- "Il n'y a plus de baguette ?" - There is no more baguette?
- "Je ne comprends pas. Pouvez-vous repeter ?" - I don't understand. Can you repeat?

**Grammar: Negation with ne...pas**

The basic French negation wraps around the conjugated verb: **ne + verb + pas**.

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|---------|
| Je parle francais. | Je **ne** parle **pas** francais. |
| Il a un billet. | Il **n'**a **pas** de billet. |
| Nous comprenons. | Nous **ne** comprenons **pas**. |
| Elle mange de la viande. | Elle **ne** mange **pas de** viande. |

Note: **ne** becomes **n'** before a vowel or silent h.
Note: after ne...pas, partitive and indefinite articles (du, de la, un, une, des) become **de / d'**.

**Other negation patterns (same ne...X structure):**
- ne...jamais (never): "Je ne mange jamais de viande."
- ne...plus (no more): "Il n'y a plus de pain."
- ne...rien (nothing): "Je ne comprends rien."
- ne...personne (nobody): "Il n'y a personne ici."

## Chapter 6: Toulouse
**Story:** The traveler heads west to Toulouse, "La Ville Rose" built from pink terracotta brick, home of Airbus and one of France's largest student populations. They need to navigate the metro and ask locals for help.

**Vocabulary Objectives:** Public transport, places in the city, asking for directions
**Grammar Objective:** Question formation (est-ce que, question words, intonation)

---

### Lesson 6.1: Taking the Metro (Prendre le Metro)

**Vocabulary**

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| le metro | metro / underground |
| le bus | bus |
| le tramway | tram |
| l'arret (m) | stop (bus/tram) |
| la station | metro station |
| la ligne | line |
| le plan | map |
| composter | to validate (a ticket) |
| un carnet | book of 10 tickets |
| la correspondance | connection / transfer |
| changer de ligne | to change lines |
| prochain(e) | next |
| le terminus | end of the line |
| direct | direct (no changes) |
| combien de temps ? | how long? |

**Key Phrases**
- "Est-ce qu'il y a un metro a Toulouse ?" - Is there a metro in Toulouse?
- "C'est quelle ligne pour le centre-ville ?" - Which line is it for the city centre?
- "Il faut changer ?" - Do I need to change?
- "Le prochain bus passe a quelle heure ?" - What time does the next bus come?
- "N'oubliez pas de composter votre billet !" - Don't forget to validate your ticket!

**Grammar: Question Formation - 3 Ways**

In French, there are three ways to ask a yes/no question:

**1. Intonation (rising voice) - informal:**
- "Tu parles francais ?" - Do you speak French?
- "Il y a un bus ?" - Is there a bus?

**2. est-ce que... - neutral, common in everyday speech:**
- "Est-ce que tu parles francais ?"
- "Est-ce qu'il y a un bus ?" (est-ce que becomes est-ce qu' before a vowel)

**3. Inversion - formal / written:**
- "Parlez-vous francais ?"
- "Y a-t-il un bus ?" (-t- is inserted between two vowels)

Use **est-ce que** in everyday speech. Use inversion in formal writing or with common short verbs.

**Grammar: Reflexive Verbs (Verbes Pronominaux)**

Reflexive verbs use a reflexive pronoun that refers back to the subject. The pronoun goes BEFORE the verb.

| s'appeler (to be called) | | se lever (to get up) | |
|---------|--|---------|--|
| je m'appelle | I am called | je me leve | I get up |

| nous nous appelons | we are called | nous nous levons | we get up |
| vous vous appelez | you are called | vous vous levez | you get up |
| ils/elles s'appellent | they are called | ils/elles se levent | they get up |

Reflexive pronouns: me (m'), te (t'), se (s'), nous, vous, se (s')

**Negation with reflexive verbs:** ne goes BEFORE the reflexive pronoun, pas goes AFTER the verb.
- "Je **ne** me **leve pas** tot." - I don't get up early.
- "Il **ne** s'**appelle pas** Pierre." - His name is not Pierre.

**Grammar: Aller + Infinitive (Le Futur Proche)**

The most common way to express the near future in French: conjugate **aller** (to go) in the present tense, then add an infinitive.

| aller | |
|-------|--|
| je vais | I am going (to) |
| tu vas | you are going (to) |
| il/elle va | he/she is going (to) |
| nous allons | we are going (to) |
| vous allez | you are going (to) |
| ils/elles vont | they are going (to) |

- "Je **vais visiter** la Grand-Place ce soir." - I am going to visit the Grand-Place tonight.
- "Nous **allons manger** des moules-frites." - We are going to eat moules-frites.
- "Il **va acheter** du chocolat." - He is going to buy some chocolate.

**Negative near future:** ne + aller + pas + infinitive
- "Je **ne vais pas partir** demain." - I am not going to leave tomorrow.

**Question near future:**
- "**Est-ce que tu vas visiter** le Manneken Pis ?" - Are you going to visit the Manneken Pis?

**What an A1 French speaker can now do:**
- Greet people and introduce themselves in formal and informal situations
- Ask simple questions using est-ce que and question words
- Order food and drinks in a restaurant or cafe
- Buy things at a market or shop and understand prices
- Ask for and understand basic directions
- Describe objects and places using adjectives correctly
- Talk about daily routines using reflexive verbs
- Understand and use numbers up to 100
- Tell and ask about the time using 12h and 24h formats
- Talk about near-future plans using aller + infinitive
- Use polite forms (je voudrais, vous pouvez) in practical situations

**Regional accent note (France + Belgium):**
- **Alsace (Strasbourg):** slight German influence, rolled R, clearer vowels
- **South (Lyon, Nice, Marseille, Toulouse, Bordeaux):** the "accent du Midi" - final silent "e" is often pronounced, speech is warmer and more melodic, nasal vowels more open
- **Paris:** the standard accent used in schools and media
- **Lille:** influenced by Picard (Ch'ti dialect) - famously showcased in the film "Bienvenue chez les Ch'tis"
- **Brussels (Belgian French):** slightly different intonation; uses "septante" (70), "huitante" (80), "nonante" (90) instead of the complex French counting system

**What comes next at A2:**
- Passe compose (past tense for speaking): "J'ai mange, je suis alle..."
- Imparfait (descriptive past): "C'etait beau, il faisait chaud..."
- Indirect object pronouns: lui, leur
- Comparative and superlative: plus grand que, le plus beau
- Prepositions with countries: en France, au Portugal, aux Etats-Unis
- More irregular verbs: savoir, connaitre, mettre, prendre, venir
